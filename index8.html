ABOUT GENEVA:



Geneva is a Swiss city located at the end of Lake Leman. It is surrounded by the Jura mountains and the Alps. It offers a magnificent view of Mont-Blanc. It is one of the most populated cities in Switzerland and the economic capital. Geneva is well known for its banks and banking secrecy. Geneva is also one of the cities that hosts the most international organizations such as the UN, WTO, CERN etc. One of the emblems is of course its magnificent water jet.

The Geneva water jet is 140 meters high and was created in 1891. It is the emblem of the city.

GENEVA AND ITS CHOCOLATE

Geneva is well known for its luxury chocolates. Indeed, we can find the best chocolate makers in the world. We can find Barry Cabellaut, Chocolate curd, Suchard and finally Laderach who won the prize for the best chocolate in 2018. Geneva also offers a visit of the 5 senses of chocolate, which can be a very interesting tourist activity.

GENEVA AND ITS CHEESE

Geneva is also well known for its taste of cheese. There is a lot of gastronomy around the cheese. We find Gruyère, Vacherin, Plateau d'Appenzeller, Emmental cheese.... As you may know, one of the specialities that everyone loves is Swiss fondue. (also called half and half fondue) This one is based on vacherin and gruyère. These cheeses are melted in a pot rubbed with garlic and white wine. This fondue is served with bread cut into large cubes.

GENEVA AND ITS WEALTH

Geneva is also well known for its luxury shopping. We can find all the biggest brands of jewellery, leather goods and ready-to-wear in the city centre and more precisely in the rue du rhone. The world's leading watch brands are Swiss. We can find Jaeger-LeCoultre, Breitling, Rolex, Patek Philippe etc. We can also find very large palaces such as the Beau-Rivage or the kempinski. As mentioned earlier, the GDP of Geneva as well as that of Switzerland is very high. There is also very little unemployment in Geneva and wages are much higher. They are generally at least 2 or 3 times higher than a French salary. This gives the population a very high purchasing power. Don't be surprised to see an 18-year-old child having an Aston Martin as his first car. This is normal in Geneva. The mentality towards the agent is quite different from that of its neighbouring countries.
